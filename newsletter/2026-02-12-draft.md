## What's Moving the Market

### Jobs Report Beats Expectations, But Fed Stays Put

The January jobs report finally arrived after delays from the government shutdown, exceeding expectations with 130,000 jobs addedâ€”more than double the 55,000 estimate. Unemployment edged down to 4.3% from 4.4%. ### Senior Home Equity Hits Record $14.66 Trillion as Trump Opposes Price Declines

Housing wealth held by homeowners age 62 and older climbed to a record $14.66 trillion in Q3 2025, a 1.9% quarterly increase. President Trump made his position clear: he doesn't want housing prices to fall, prioritizing existing homeowners who've benefited from appreciation over younger buyers locked out of tight markets. In 2024, 81% of Trump voters were homeowners. Research shows sellers in their 70s and older consistently receive lower returnsâ€”about 0.5% less per year, or roughly $20,000 less on a $400,000 home over an 11-year hold.

Why it matters:

Older sellers prioritize speed and simplicity over maximum value, selling off-MLS and taking first offers more often. If you're positioned to provide certainty and a clean close, that's your edge.

## What's Worth Watching

### Trump Administration Explores "Trump Homes" Rent-to-Own Program

Shares of Lennar jumped last week after Bloomberg reported the builder is exploring a large-scale rent-to-own program dubbed "Trump Homes." The concept: builders sell newly built entry-level homes to private investors, who rent them to tenants with part of the monthly rent credited toward a future down payment. In some versions, renters could purchase after about three years. Bloomberg reports the program could eventually involve up to one million homes, though the White House hasn't formally endorsed the plan.

Why it matters:

If scaled, this creates a new institutional asset class and opens capital deployment for operators who can structure deals with builders. It's an access solution, not a demand solutionâ€”exactly what builders need in a high-rate environment.

Source: https://udirectira.com/trump-401k-down-payment-proposal-retirement-detour/

The U.S. apartment market saw net move-outs for the first time in three years in Q4 2025, according to RealPage dataâ€”a return to typical seasonal patterns rather than a breakdown. Columbus was the lone Midwest market with positive demand (~500 units).

### Single-Family Construction Permits Fall 9.4% as Builders Pull Back

Single-family construction permits declined 9.4% over the past year to an annual rate of 876,000, according to the U.S. Census Bureau. The slowdown reflects tighter financing, elevated costs, and weaker buyer demand. Even as senior housing wealth hits records and the labor market stabilizes, builders aren't rushing to add supply. The pullback is most pronounced in overbuilt Sunbelt markets where inventory surged in 2023-2024.

Why it matters:

Less supply today means tighter inventory 12-18 months from now. Builders don't reverse course quicklyâ€”today's pullback impacts 2026-2027 availability, creating a narrowing window to acquire quality inventory before supply constraints push pricing higher.
